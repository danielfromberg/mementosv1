    my sleep poem

    By Talia Wright


    What does it mean to hold a body?

    my precious one of dusk &

    quiet, call & come & tell me, what

    do they say about beginnings? Karmic

    circles and solar returns? Your left

    foot entangled around my right

    calf. A galaxy of skin-to-skin like

    night takes all, like we are gravitationally

    bound, like boundless, like flight, like

    asleep, like taking off. Like I’m the girl on

    rocket ship behaving like a meteorite

    because asteroids have shoes too big to fill

Talia Wright (they/she) was born and raised on the Southside of Chicago. They are a poetry/prose writer and 2019 Pink Door Fellow. They have been published in In These Times, Changing Womxn, Beestung Magazine, and more. Their work is informed by blackness, the great migration, and spending summer afternoons dancing under their grandparents’ Mulberry tree. Follow them on instagram @cherub.jpeg
